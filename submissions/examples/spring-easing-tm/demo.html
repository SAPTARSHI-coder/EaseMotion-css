Understanding Spring Easing

Spring easing (or elastic easing) mimics the physical movement of a spring. Unlike standard linear or ease curves, it accounts for momentum and settling time.

  • Underdamped: The object overshoots the target and "bounces" or oscillates back and forth before settling. Great for playful, energetic UI.
  • Critically Damped: The fastest possible motion that reaches the target without any overshoot. Perfect for high-performance, snappy menus.
  • Overdamped: A heavy, slow motion that avoids overshoot but takes longer to reach equilibrium. Ideal for subtle, sophisticated transitions.
