CSS :nth-child(an+b of .scoped)

Scoped structural selectors that count only matching elements among siblings.

How it works

Regular :nth-child(2) selects the second child among all siblings. With :nth-child(2 of .visible), it selects the second element that matches .visible — skipping hidden elements entirely.

Scoped Selectors in Action

First visible item (scoped)

.gallery-item:first-child always targets the first DOM child. But .gallery-item:nth-child(1 of .visible) targets the first visible item after filtering — even if it's the 3rd child in the DOM.

Alternating visible items

Try filtering to "Web" — the :nth-child(odd of .visible) and :nth-child(even of .visible) selectors will target the 1st, 3rd, 5th and 2nd, 4th visible items respectively.
