Frequently Asked Questions

Is microwave energy transmission safe for humans and wildlife?

Yes. The microwave beams operate at a low power density (about 1/4 the intensity of natural sunlight). Birds and aircraft can pass through the beam safely without harm. The system also includes automatic shutoff mechanisms if physical obstructions are detected.

How does weather affect transmission?

We use specific microwave frequencies (typically around 2.45 GHz or 5.8 GHz) that pass through clouds, rain, and snow with minimal energy loss. Unlike ground-based solar, our orbital system provides consistent power regardless of terrestrial weather.

What is a rectenna?

A rectenna (rectifying antenna) is a specialized antenna used to convert microwave energy directly into direct current (DC) electricity. They form the ground-based receiver grid and can be built over agricultural land or bodies of water.

How are the satellites maintained in orbit?

The satellites are highly modular and autonomous. They use a portion of the solar energy they collect to power ion thrusters for station-keeping. Automated repair drones in orbit handle component replacements.
